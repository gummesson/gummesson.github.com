About

My name is Ellen Gummesson and I love communication and the web. I recently recieved a degree in media and communication studies, specialising in strategic communication. Lately I’ve been diving straight into front-end development, mainly writing HTML5 and CSS3, dabbling a little bit with jQuery and starting to really enjoy Javascript. I also like Sass and love Compass.

I’ve also started to learn the programming language Ruby and I’m hoping that one day I’ll conquer the Ruby on Rails framework. In the meantime I’m having fun with micro web framework Sinatra. I enjoy writing small but practically useless Bash scripts that I run on my Manjaro Linux box. You can check out my projects on Gitub if you’d like, although I’m still very much a beginner when it comes to coding.

Why are you writing this in english?

I see it both as a fun challenge and an learning experience. English is a pretty important language and even though I think I’m pretty good at both speaking writing it there’s always room for improvement.

Colophon

This site is powered by Jekyll and hosted on GitHub Pages. It was built with HTML5, CSS3 and Sass with Compass. The code was written by hand in Vim with the colorscheme Tomorrow Night Eighties. The site makes use of the rather nice looking typefaces Noto Sans and Sanchez, which are provided by Google Webfonts.

The blog posts are written in Markdown, scribbled down in Pop, iA Writer or ResophNotes before finally ending up and being finished in FocusWriter, Vim or occasionally in MarkdownPad. Shotty is used for taking the screenshots.
