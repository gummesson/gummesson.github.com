About

My name is Ellen Gummesson and I love communication and the web. I recently recieved a degree in media and communication studies, specialising in strategic communication. I previously worked as a writer and reviewer for a reasonably large magazine. Lately I’ve been diving straight into front-end development, mainly writing HTML5, CSS3 and dabbling a little bit with jQuery. I also like Sass and love Compass.

I’ve also started to learn the programming language Ruby and I’m hoping that one day I’ll conquer the Ruby on Rails framework. In the meantime I’m having fun with micro web framework Sinatra. I enjoy writing small but practically useless Bash scripts that I run on my Manjaro Linux box. You can check out my projects if you’d like, although I’m still very much a beginner when it comes to coding.

Why are you writing this in english?

I see it both as a fun challenge and an learning experience. English is a pretty important language and even though I think I’m pretty good at both speaking writing it there’s always room for improvement.

Colophon

This site is powered by Jekyll and hosted on GitHub Pages. It was built with HTML5 and Sass with Compass on top of Smallwork. The code was written by hand in Vim with the colorscheme Solarized. The site makes use of the rather nice looking typeface Roboto, which is provided by Google Webfonts. The colors come from Noah Frederick’s beautiful terminal theme Peppermint.

The blog posts are written in Markdown, scribbled down in Pop, iA Writer or ResophNotes before finally ending up and being finished in FocusWriter, Vim or occasionally in MarkdownPad. Shotty is used for taking the screenshots.
